## Learning Objectives (Bloom's Taxonomy)

### Understand & Apply
1. Apply strategic communication frameworks to achieve specific business outcomes (know-feel-do)
2. Analyze diverse audiences to adapt message content, structure, and delivery
3. Implement evidence-based writing principles for clarity, concision, and persuasive impact
4. Utilize appropriate communication channels and platforms for different business contexts
5. **Apply AI tools appropriately for business communication tasks with human oversight**

### Analyze & Evaluate
6. Evaluate the effectiveness of business communications across written, oral, and digital formats
7. Assess communicator credibility and identify strategies to build trust and authority
8. Analyze cultural and organizational factors that impact communication effectiveness
9. Critique message structure, visual design, and delivery for strategic alignment with goals
10. **Evaluate when AI is appropriate vs inappropriate for communication tasks**

### Create & Present
11. Compose professional business documents (emails, memos, reports, proposals)
12. Design and deliver compelling presentations with clear structure and visual clarity
13. Develop an authentic communication style that balances confidence, credibility, and connection
14. Create persuasive messages that overcome resistance and drive action
15. **Produce AI-assisted content with quality control and authentic voice**

### Collaborate
16. Provide and receive constructive feedback to improve communication effectiveness
17. Navigate difficult conversations including disagreements, accountability, and conflict resolution
18. Practice active listening and contribute productively to collaborative decision-making
19. **Collaborate effectively in virtual team environments**

## 10-Week Course Structure

### Week 1: Strategic Communication Foundations
**Topic**: Communication process in modern business contexts
**Key Concepts**:
- Communication process model (sender, message, receiver, feedback, noise)
- Audience analysis framework
- Know-feel-do outcomes framework
- Workplace communication channels

**Aligns with course description**: "strategic communication competencies"

---

### Week 2: AI Tools for Professional Writing
**Topic**: Practical guidance on when and how to use AI in business communication
**Key Concepts**:
- **When to use AI**: Brainstorming, outlining, revision, routine communications
- **When NOT to use AI**: Confidential data, strategic decisions, relationship-critical messages
- **How to use AI effectively**: Prompt engineering (PTCF framework), quality control, human oversight
- **Algorithm aversion**: Understanding credibility challenges with AI-generated content
- Writing fundamentals: Clarity, concision, professional tone

**Aligns with course description**: "emerging technologies (including AI)"

**Research needed**: Comprehensive AI guidance from May-Nov 2025 sources

---

### Week 3: Digital Communication & Virtual Collaboration
**Topic**: Professional communication across digital channels and virtual teams
**Key Concepts**:
- Email best practices and etiquette
- Instant messaging and Slack/Teams communication
- **Virtual collaboration tools and strategies**
- **Asynchronous vs synchronous communication**
- Building presence in remote/hybrid environments

**Aligns with course description**: "virtual collaboration" + "digital communication"

---

### Week 4: Positive and Neutral Messages
**Topic**: Direct approach for routine business communications
**Key Concepts**:
- Direct organizational pattern
- Goodwill and relationship building
- Routine requests and responses
- Professional courtesy across cultures

**Aligns with course description**: "diverse cultural contexts"

---

### Week 5: Bad News & Crisis Communication
**Topic**: Delivering difficult messages and managing crises
**Key Concepts**:
- Indirect approach and buffer strategies
- Maintaining relationships while delivering bad news
- **Crisis communication frameworks**
- **Stakeholder management in high-stress situations**
- Transparency and accountability

**Aligns with course description**: "crisis management"

---

### Week 6: Persuasive Communication & Ethics
**Topic**: Influence and persuasion with ethical boundaries
**Key Concepts**:
- Persuasion frameworks (Cialdini's principles, AIDA model)
- Building credibility and trust
- Call to action strategies
- **Ethical persuasion boundaries**
- Overcoming resistance

**Aligns with course description**: "ethical communication in a globalized business world"

---

### Week 7: Data Storytelling & Visual Communication
**Topic**: Communicating insights through data narratives
**Key Concepts**:
- **Data storytelling frameworks**
- Data visualization best practices
- Report structure and organization
- Translating complex data for non-technical audiences
- Visual design principles

**Aligns with course description**: "data storytelling" (explicit)

---

### Week 8: Business Presentations
**Topic**: Designing and delivering compelling presentations
**Key Concepts**:
- Presentation structure (opening, body, closing)
- Visual aid design
- Audience engagement techniques
- Delivery confidence and presence
- Q&A management

**Aligns with course description**: "deliver presentations"

---

### Week 9: Cross-Cultural & Global Communication
**Topic**: Communication effectiveness in diverse cultural contexts
**Key Concepts**:
- **Cultural intelligence frameworks** (Hofstede, Meyer)
- High-context vs low-context cultures
- **Global business communication practices**
- Adapting messages for international audiences
- Team dynamics in diverse groups

**Aligns with course description**: "diverse cultural and organizational contexts" + "globalized business world" + "cultural intelligence"

---

### Week 10: Professional Presence & Career Communication
**Topic**: Building professional identity and career advancement
**Key Concepts**:
- Professional branding and networking
- LinkedIn and digital presence
- Career documents (résumés, cover letters)
- Interview communication
- Ongoing professional development

**Aligns with course description**: "professional success in the modern business environment"
