## Proposed 10-Week Structure

Based on synthesis of top school approaches and Guffey textbook:

**Week 1:** Strategic Communication Foundations
- Communication process, digital workplace, audience analysis
- **Key concepts:** Communication process, audience analysis, workplace contexts

**Week 2:** Professional Writing Fundamentals
- Planning, organizing, and drafting effective messages
- **Key concepts:** Writing process, message structure, clarity and concision

**Week 3:** Digital and Short-Form Communication
- Emails, memos, instant messaging, social media for business
- **Key concepts:** Digital channels, professional tone, brevity

**Week 4:** Positive and Neutral Messages
- Routine requests, replies, goodwill messages
- **Key concepts:** Direct approach, positive messaging, professional courtesy

**Week 5:** Delivering Bad News
- Indirect approach, buffer strategies, maintaining goodwill
- **Key concepts:** Indirect approach, buffer, maintaining relationships

**Week 6:** Persuasive Communication
- Persuasion frameworks (AIDA), overcoming resistance, proposals
- **Key concepts:** Persuasion principles, call to action, credibility

**Week 7:** Business Reports and Data Communication
- Informal reports, data visualization, storytelling with data
- **Key concepts:** Report structure, data interpretation, visual design

**Week 8:** Business Presentations
- Presentation structure, visual aids, delivery techniques
- **Key concepts:** Presentation design, audience engagement, delivery confidence

**Week 9:** Cross-Cultural and Team Communication
- Cultural intelligence, virtual teams, collaboration tools
- **Key concepts:** Cultural dimensions, team dynamics, conflict resolution

**Week 10:** Professional Presence and Career Communication
- Business etiquette, networking, personal branding, job search communication
- **Key concepts:** Professional identity, networking strategies, career documents

## Synthesis - Learning Objectives by Bloom's Taxonomy

### Understand & Apply (Foundation level)
- Apply strategic communication frameworks to achieve specific business outcomes (know-feel-do)
- Analyze diverse audiences to adapt message content, structure, and delivery
- Implement evidence-based writing principles for clarity, concision, and persuasive impact
- Utilize appropriate communication channels and platforms for different business contexts

### Analyze & Evaluate (Critical thinking level)
- Evaluate the effectiveness of business communications across written, oral, and digital formats
- Assess communicator credibility and identify strategies to build trust and authority
- Analyze cultural and organizational factors that impact communication effectiveness
- Critique message structure, visual design, and delivery for strategic alignment with goals

### Create & Present (Production level)
- Compose professional business documents (emails, memos, reports, proposals)
- Design and deliver compelling presentations with clear structure and visual clarity
- Develop an authentic communication style that balances confidence, credibility, and connection
- Create persuasive messages that overcome resistance and drive action

### Collaborate (Interpersonal level)
- Provide and receive constructive feedback to improve communication effectiveness
- Navigate difficult conversations including disagreements, accountability, and conflict resolution
- Practice active listening and contribute productively to collaborative decision-making
