# Course Description Research - Business Communication

## Research Date
January 24, 2025

## Schools Researched

### Harvard Business School
- **Course:** The Arts of Communication (7515)
- **Key Elements:**
  - Focus on practical scenarios: feedback, conflict, influence
  - Emphasizes confidence, presence, trust, credibility
  - Storytelling and managing high-stress situations
  - Beyond formal speeches to everyday leadership communication

### Wharton (UPenn)
- **Course:** Business Communication for Impact (WH 2010)
- **Key Elements:**
  - All communication types: spoken/written, formal/informal, prepared/spontaneous
  - Audience analysis and outcome-based thinking
  - Persuasion, concision, clarity, visual communication
  - Employers value soft skills (65%) over technical skills (50%)

### MIT Sloan
- **Course:** Communication for Leaders + Managerial Communication Unit
- **Key Elements:**
  - Theory and skill development across: oral, written, interpersonal, cross-cultural, leadership
  - Communication as foundational leadership capability
  - Strategic communication to influence organizational culture
  - Integrated throughout MBA curriculum

## Common Themes Across Top Schools

1. **Strategic Communication:** Not just speaking/writing, but achieving specific outcomes
2. **Multi-Modal:** Written, oral, digital, interpersonal contexts
3. **Practical Application:** Hands-on exercises, feedback, real-world scenarios
4. **Leadership Context:** Communication as essential leadership skill
5. **Cultural Awareness:** Cross-cultural and diverse audience considerations
6. **Career Impact:** Communication skills differentiate high performers

## Course Description Structure

**Paragraph 1: Purpose and Foundation**
- Strategic communication competencies for professional success
- Multi-modal: written, oral, digital, interpersonal
- Diverse cultural and organizational contexts

**Paragraph 2: Pedagogical Approach**
- Hands-on application
- Portfolio of professional documents
- Presentations and real business challenges
- Emerging technologies (AI), data storytelling, virtual collaboration

**Paragraph 3: Value Proposition**
- Clarity, cultural intelligence, strategic impact
- Skills that distinguish high performers
- Digital and interconnected marketplace preparation
