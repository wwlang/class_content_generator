# Presentation Rubric Template

Use this template for individual or group presentations, including business presentations, research presentations, and pitch presentations.

---

## **Presentation Rubric**

| Criteria | Excellent (94-100%) | Good (84-93%) | Satisfactory (74-83%) | Poor (60-73%) | Failing (Below 60%) |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Content Quality** | Compelling message with strong evidence; excellent depth and insight; sophisticated analysis | Good content with solid support; adequate depth; clear understanding | Basic content; some support; surface-level treatment but acceptable | Weak content; insufficient support; very superficial; limited understanding | Poor content; no real support; no depth; no understanding |
| **Organization & Structure** | Masterful storytelling structure; perfect flow and transitions; engaging narrative arc | Clear structure with good narrative flow; logical progression; good transitions | Adequate structure; some flow issues but generally logical | Weak structure; hard to follow; poor transitions; confusing | No clear structure; disorganized and confusing |
| **Delivery** | Confident, engaging, natural; excellent eye contact and body language; commands attention | Good delivery with minor hesitations; good presence; maintains attention | Adequate delivery; reads notes frequently; somewhat nervous but acceptable | Poor delivery; excessive reading; very nervous; struggles to engage | Ineffective delivery; no engagement; completely reads; no presence |
| **Visual Aids** | Slides enhance message perfectly; professional, polished design; follows best practices | Good slides that support message; clean, clear design; mostly follows best practices | Basic slides; some design issues; generally readable; acceptable quality | Poor slides; major design problems; hard to read; distracts from message | Slides detract from message or missing; unprofessional; unreadable |
| **Time Management & Q&A** | Perfect timing (within 30 seconds); excellent Q&A handling with confidence and knowledge | Within time limit; handles Q&A well; demonstrates good knowledge | Slightly over/under time (by 1-2 min); Q&A adequate; reasonable responses | Significantly off time (by 3+ min); struggles with Q&A; weak responses | Way off time; cannot handle Q&A; no knowledge demonstrated |

---

## Customization Instructions

When adapting this rubric for specific presentations:

1. **Adjust criteria weights** based on presentation type (e.g., content may be 40% for research presentations)
2. **Add specific criteria** for specialized presentations
3. **Modify time expectations** based on assignment requirements
4. **Clarify visual aid requirements** (PowerPoint, Prezi, physical props, etc.)

## Example Customizations

### For Technical/Research Presentations:
- Add criterion: "Technical Accuracy & Depth"
- Add criterion: "Use of Data/Evidence"
- Increase weight of "Content Quality"

### For Persuasive/Pitch Presentations:
- Add criterion: "Persuasive Impact"
- Add criterion: "Call to Action Clarity"
- Modify "Content Quality" to emphasize problem-solution structure

### For Group Presentations:
- Add section for "Team Coordination"
- Add criterion: "Equitable Participation"
- Consider separate individual grades within group grade

## Individual Contribution Rubric (for Group Presentations)

| Criteria | Excellent (94-100%) | Good (84-93%) | Satisfactory (74-83%) | Poor (60-73%) | Failing (Below 60%) |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Individual Preparation** | Thoroughly prepared; clearly practiced; confident with material | Well-prepared; practiced; comfortable with content | Adequately prepared; some hesitation but acceptable | Under-prepared; frequent hesitation; lacks confidence | Not prepared; cannot deliver content effectively |
| **Quality of Section** | Section is exemplary; adds significant value to overall presentation | Section is strong; contributes well to presentation | Section is adequate; meets minimum requirements | Section is weak; detracts from overall quality | Section is poor; significantly hurts presentation |
| **Team Contribution** | Peer evaluations show outstanding contribution; went above and beyond | Peer evaluations show strong contribution; completed fair share | Peer evaluations show adequate contribution; did assigned work | Peer evaluations show limited contribution; did minimum | Peer evaluations show little to no contribution |
