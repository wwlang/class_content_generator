## Research Methodology

### 4-Stage Process Used:

**Stage 1: Discovery (10-15 candidates)**
- Search top schools: HBS, Stanford GSB, Wharton, MIT Sloan
- Search academic: Google Scholar, peer-reviewed journals, open access
- Search practitioner: HBR, MIT Sloan Review, WEF
- Identify 10-15 candidate articles per week

**Stage 2: Quick Filter (5-6 candidates)**
- Check accessibility (open access preferred, seminal paywalled acceptable)
- Verify quality (peer-reviewed, top-tier publishers, highly cited)
- Review relevance to topic
- Reduce to 5-6 candidates

**Stage 3: Content Validation (2-3 finalists)**
- Deep analysis of content against required concepts
- STRICT: ALL required concepts must be covered adequately
- Depth check: "Explained" or "In-depth" coverage required
- Reduce to 2-3 finalists that pass validation

**Stage 4: Final Selection (2 articles)**
- Present finalists with content match analysis
- Select final 2 articles (ideally 1 theoretical + 1 applied)
- Ensure complementary coverage across both articles

### Quality Standards:

**Acceptable Sources:**
- ✓ Peer-reviewed academic journals
- ✓ Top-tier practitioner sources (HBR, MIT SMR, McKinsey)
- ✓ Authoritative reports (WEF, OECD, top universities)
- ✓ Seminal/highly cited works (even if paywalled)
- ✓ Open access preferred but not required

**Validation Criteria:**
- ✓ ALL required concepts must be covered
- ✓ Coverage depth: "Explained" or "In-depth" minimum
- ✓ Current (2020-2025) or proven seminal
- ✓ Appropriate for undergraduate business students
- ✓ Practical applications included
