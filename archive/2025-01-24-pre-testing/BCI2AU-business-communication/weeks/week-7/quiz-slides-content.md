**SLIDE 2: Question 1 - Hofstede's Cultural Dimensions**

TITLE: Question 1: Hofstede's Cultural Dimensions

CONTENT:

**Scenario:** You're managing a project team with members from Vietnam (high power distance, collectivist) and the United States (low power distance, individualist). During meetings, Vietnamese team members rarely speak up or challenge ideas, while American members are very vocal. According to Hofstede's cultural dimensions framework, which dimension best explains this dynamic?

**A)** Uncertainty Avoidance

**B)** Power Distance

**C)** Masculinity vs. Femininity

**D)** Long-term Orientation

---

## Speaker Notes

Give students 2 minutes to consider. This tests understanding of Hofstede's Power Distance dimension and its impact on communication styles in hierarchical vs. egalitarian cultures.

---

**SLIDE 3: Question 2 - High-Context vs. Low-Context Cultures**

TITLE: Question 2: High-Context vs. Low-Context Cultures

CONTENT:

**Scenario:** You're writing an email to a Japanese business partner (high-context culture) to decline a partnership proposal. According to Meyer's framework on high-context vs. low-context communication, which approach is most appropriate?

**A)** State directly: "We cannot move forward with this partnership."

**B)** Explain: "After careful consideration, we've decided to pursue other opportunities at this time."

**C)** Suggest: "We appreciate your proposal and will need more time to evaluate how this fits with our current strategic priorities."

**D)** Apologize profusely: "We're so sorry, but this partnership won't work for us right now."

---

## Speaker Notes

Give students 2 minutes to consider. This tests understanding of Meyer's high-context vs. low-context communication framework and appropriate indirectness for high-context cultures like Japan.

---

**SLIDE 4: Question 3 - Managing Multicultural Teams**

TITLE: Question 3: Managing Multicultural Teams

CONTENT:

According to Brett, Behfar, and Kern's research on managing multicultural teams, which of the following is the MOST effective intervention when cultural differences cause team conflict?

**A)** Remove the team member causing the problem

**B)** Exit the team entirely and reform with homogeneous membership

**C)** Adaptation: Acknowledge cultural differences and work around them

**D)** Ignore the differences and focus only on the task

---

## Speaker Notes

Give students 2 minutes to consider. This tests understanding of Brett et al.'s four strategies for managing multicultural teams: Adaptation, Structural Intervention, Managerial Intervention, and Exit.

**SLIDE 5: Question 4 - Ethical AI Use**

TITLE: Question 4: Ethical AI Use

CONTENT:

**Scenario:** You're writing a persuasive proposal for your Written Portfolio assignment. You use ChatGPT to generate three different versions of your opening paragraph, then select the best one and revise it to match your personal writing style. According to the course AI policy, is this acceptable?

**A)** Yes, this is appropriate use of AI for brainstorming and idea generation

**B)** No, you cannot use AI for any part of graded assignments

**C)** Yes, but only if you disclose this AI use in your assignment submission

**D)** No, because you're submitting AI-generated content as your own work

---

## Speaker Notes

Give students 2 minutes to consider. This tests understanding of the course AI policy, particularly the disclosure requirement for AI use beyond grammar checking.

**SLIDE 6: Question 5 - AI and Productivity**

TITLE: Question 5: AI and Productivity

CONTENT:

According to Brynjolfsson, Li, and Raymond's research on generative AI at work, which group saw the LARGEST productivity gains from using AI tools?

**A)** Top performers (already highly skilled)

**B)** Average performers (middle 50%)

**C)** New hires and lower-skilled workers

**D)** Managers and executives

---

## Speaker Notes

Give students 2 minutes to consider. This tests recall of Brynjolfsson et al.'s key finding that generative AI had the largest impact on lower-skilled workers (35% increase), functioning as a "leveling" technology.

**SLIDE 7: Question 6 - Maintaining Authenticity with AI**

TITLE: Question 6: Maintaining Authenticity with AI

CONTENT:

Your manager asks you to use AI to write all client emails to save time. According to course principles on AI and authenticity, what's the BEST response?

**A)** Refuse entirely—AI has no place in professional communication

**B)** Agree and use AI to draft all emails without editing

**C)** Suggest using AI for drafts, but personally review and revise each email to maintain authentic voice and accuracy

**D)** Use AI but don't tell clients

---

## Speaker Notes

Give students 2 minutes to consider. This tests understanding of AI as a tool (not replacement) and the importance of authenticity, accuracy verification, and ethical transparency in professional communication.

**SLIDE 9: Question 8 - Speed vs. Accuracy in Crisis**

TITLE: Question 8: Speed vs. Accuracy in Crisis

CONTENT:

Your company's website crashes during a major product launch. You don't yet know the cause. According to McNulty's guidance on crisis communication, what should you communicate in the first hour?

**A)** Wait until you have complete information before saying anything

**B)** Blame the IT team to deflect responsibility

**C)** Acknowledge the issue, explain what you're doing to investigate, and commit to updates

**D)** Provide a detailed technical explanation of potential causes

---

## Speaker Notes

Give students 2 minutes to consider. This tests understanding of McNulty's principle that speed matters in crisis communication—you must acknowledge and communicate quickly even with limited information.
