# BCI2AU - Business Communication
## Article Research Summary - Fall 2025

This document contains the complete research process and final article selections for all 11 weeks of the course.

**Research Method:** 4-stage validation process
- Stage 1: Discovery (10-15 candidates per week)
- Stage 2: Quick filter (accessibility check)
- Stage 3: Content validation (ALL required concepts must be covered)
- Stage 4: Final selection

**Research Sources:** HBS, Stanford GSB, Wharton, MIT Sloan, peer-reviewed journals, HBR, MIT Sloan Management Review

## WEEK 1: Strategic Communication Foundations
**Required Concepts:** Communication models, audience analysis, goal-driven messaging (know-feel-do), credibility building

### SELECTED ARTICLES:

**Article 1:**
Hallenbeck, G., & Childs, P. (2022, March 9). To win over an audience, focus on building trust. *Harvard Business Review*. https://hbr.org/2022/03/to-win-over-an-audience-focus-on-building-trust

**Content Match:** ✓ Audience analysis, ✓ Credibility building (5 competencies: clarity, compassion, competency, connection, consistency), ✓ Goal-driven messaging, ✓ Strategic approach (trust precedes technique)

**Article 2:**
Hallinen, A., & Salipante, T. (2005). The strategic communication imperative. *MIT Sloan Management Review, 46*(3), 83-89. https://sloanreview.mit.edu/article/the-strategic-communication-imperative/

**Content Match:** ✓ Strategic communication definition (aligned with company strategy), ✓ Goal-driven approach, ✓ Communication integral to strategy

## WEEK 6: Crisis & Incident Communication
**Required Concepts:** Crisis frameworks, stakeholder management, transparency, speed vs. accuracy, post-crisis learning

### SELECTED ARTICLES:

**Article 1:**
Argenti, P. A. (2002, December). Crisis communication: Lessons from 9/11. *Harvard Business Review, 80*(12), 103–109. https://hbr.org/2002/12/crisis-communication-lessons-from-911

**Content Match:** ✓ Crisis frameworks (5 guidelines: visible leadership, channel diversification, centralized messaging, contingency planning, values-based improvisation), ✓ Stakeholder management (internal, external, media, unions), ✓ Transparency (multiple examples), ✓ Speed vs. accuracy (daily updates with verified info), ✓ Post-crisis learning (sustained engagement, measurement, cultural foundation)

**Article 2:**
McNulty, E. J. (2020, March 24). What employees need to hear from leaders in times of crisis. *MIT Sloan Management Review*. https://sloanreview.mit.edu/article/what-employees-need-to-hear-from-leaders-in-times-of-crisis/

**Content Match:** ✓ Crisis communication principles (daily communication, candid updates), ✓ Stakeholder focus (employee-centered), ✓ Transparency & trust (honest, frequent communication)

## WEEK 9: Presentations Part I - Design & Structure
**Required Concepts:** Presentation frameworks, storytelling structures, slide design, visual aids, opening/closing strategies

### SELECTED ARTICLES:

**Article 1:**
Anderson, C. (2013, June). How to give a killer presentation. *Harvard Business Review, 91*(6), 121–125. https://hbr.org/2013/06/how-to-give-a-killer-presentation
