# Written Communication Rubric Template

Use this template for essays, reports, memos, emails, and other written assignments.

---

## **Written Communication Rubric**

| Criteria | Excellent (94-100%) | Good (84-93%) | Satisfactory (74-83%) | Poor (60-73%) | Failing (Below 60%) |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Content & Strategy | Message perfectly suited to audience and purpose; highly strategic and persuasive | Message well-suited to audience and purpose; strategic approach evident | Message generally appropriate; some strategic elements present | Message somewhat unclear or poorly matched to audience/purpose | Message inappropriate or ineffective for audience/purpose |
| Organization | Exceptionally clear structure; logical flow; strong transitions; easy to follow | Clear structure with good flow and appropriate transitions | Adequate structure; some organizational issues but generally logical | Weak organization; difficult to follow; poor transitions | No clear organization; confusing structure |
| Clarity & Concision | Every word serves a purpose; crystal clear meaning; economical language | Clear and mostly concise; minor wordiness but meaning evident | Generally clear but wordy in places; some redundancy | Unclear or excessively wordy; meaning obscured | Confusing and rambling; meaning lost |
| Tone & Professionalism | Perfect professional tone throughout; audience-appropriate register | Professional tone with minor lapses; generally appropriate | Generally professional; some tone issues that don't severely impact | Inappropriate tone in several places; professionalism questioned | Unprofessional throughout; tone completely inappropriate |
| Mechanics | No errors in grammar, spelling, punctuation; polished presentation | 1-3 minor errors that don't impact comprehension | 4-7 errors that don't significantly impede understanding | 8-12 errors; some impede understanding; appears unproofread | 13+ errors; seriously impedes understanding; careless |

---

## Customization Instructions

When adapting this rubric for specific assignments:

1. **Keep the core criteria** (Content & Strategy, Organization, Clarity & Concision, Tone & Professionalism, Mechanics)
2. **Adjust criteria names** if needed for specific assignment types
3. **Modify descriptors** to match assignment-specific requirements
4. **Add assignment-specific criteria** if needed (e.g., "Use of Data/Evidence" for reports)

## Example Customizations

### For Business Emails:
- Add criterion: "Subject Line Effectiveness"
- Modify "Tone & Professionalism" to emphasize email etiquette

### For Research Reports:
- Add criterion: "Use of Evidence & Citations"
- Add criterion: "Data Visualization Quality"
- Modify "Content & Strategy" to emphasize analytical depth

### For Persuasive Memos:
- Add criterion: "Persuasive Techniques"
- Modify "Content & Strategy" to emphasize problem-solution-benefit structure
