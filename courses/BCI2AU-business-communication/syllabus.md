# **COURSE DESCRIPTION**

This course develops strategic communication competencies essential for professional success in the modern business environment. Drawing on curriculum approaches from leading business schools including Harvard, Stanford, MIT Sloan, Wharton, Columbia, Kellogg, and Chicago Booth, students will master written, oral, and interpersonal communication across diverse cultural and organizational contexts.

The course emphasizes practical application: students will compose professional business documents, deliver presentations, and engage with realistic communication challenges. The curriculum follows a progressive skill-building approach, moving from foundational writing skills through oral presentation mastery to specialized contexts including cross-cultural communication and high-stakes executive communication.

By combining foundational communication principles with evidence-based frameworks, this course prepares students to communicate with clarity, cultural intelligence, and strategic impact—competencies that distinguish high performers in an increasingly interconnected global marketplace.

# **LEARNING OBJECTIVES**

Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to:

1. **Analyze** audiences, contexts, and communication challenges to formulate effective communication strategies for any business situation
2. **Develop** clearly organized, persuasive written communications (memos, reports, executive summaries) using structured frameworks including the Pyramid Principle
3. **Deliver** confident, engaging oral presentations that achieve intended outcomes with diverse audiences
4. **Demonstrate** executive presence and authentic communication style in high-stakes business contexts
5. **Apply** principles of persuasion, storytelling, and cross-cultural awareness to influence and inspire action
6. **Evaluate** personal communication effectiveness through self-reflection and integrate feedback for continuous improvement

# **COURSE POLICIES**

## **Group Work**

- All portfolio assignments and presentations are completed in groups of 2-4 members
- Groups must be formed by **Week 3** and remain consistent throughout the semester
- Inform instructor of group composition via email by end of Week 3 for LMS records

## **Participation Policy (10%)**

**Attendance (5%):**
- Attendance is mandatory and taken within the first 10 minutes of each class
- Students arriving after attendance is marked absent
- 2 absences allowed without penalty; each additional absence reduces participation grade

**Active Participation (5%):**
- Complete all in-class quizzes and activities
- Deductions for incomplete or missing in-class work
- Professional conduct during class sessions

## **Academic Integrity**

All students must maintain the highest standards of academic honesty. Violations include:

- **Plagiarism:** Using others' words or ideas without proper citation
- **Cheating:** Unauthorized collaboration or use of materials during assessments
- **Fabrication:** Making up data, sources, or information

**Consequences:** Violations will result in a zero on the assignment and possible failure of the course, plus referral to university disciplinary procedures.
