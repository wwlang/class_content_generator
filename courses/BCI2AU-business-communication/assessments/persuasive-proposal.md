# Persuasive Proposal

**Course:** BCI2AU - Business Communication
**Due:** End of Week 7
**Weight:** 15%

---

## Task Overview

Develop a formal persuasive proposal (800-1100 words) for a business initiative, followed by a framework reflection (300-400 words) demonstrating how you applied persuasion frameworks to your argumentation strategy. This assignment builds on Week 4's audience analysis skills and adds persuasive argumentation techniques from Cialdini's principles and rhetorical appeals.

---

## Scenario (Choose ONE)

**Option A: Sustainability Initiative**

You are a sustainability coordinator proposing a company-wide initiative to reduce plastic waste by 50% over 2 years. Write a proposal to the Executive Committee requesting $25,000 in implementation funding.

- Apply at least 3 of Cialdini's persuasion principles
- Use rhetorical appeals (ethos, pathos, logos) strategically
- Include evidence from industry benchmarks
- Address potential objections proactively

**Option B: Technology Upgrade Proposal**

You are an IT manager proposing adoption of a new project management platform for your 200-person company. Write a proposal to senior leadership requesting approval and $40,000 budget.

- Current system is 8 years old with productivity complaints
- Competitor companies have adopted modern solutions
- Apply Problem-Solution-Benefit structure
- Use data to support your arguments

**Option C: Training Program Investment**

You are an HR specialist proposing a leadership development program for high-potential employees. Write a proposal to the CEO requesting $30,000 annual investment.

- Company has high turnover among mid-level managers
- Industry data shows leadership training ROI of 200%+
- Apply storytelling techniques to humanize the data
- Include implementation timeline and success metrics

**Option D: Create Your Own**

Propose a business initiative from your work experience or career interests. Must include:

- Clear business problem
- Specific proposal with budget/resources
- Identifiable decision-maker audience

## Requirements

**Content Requirements:**

- [ ] Clear problem statement with supporting evidence
- [ ] Specific, actionable solution with implementation steps
- [ ] Application of at least 3 Cialdini principles (explicitly identified)
- [ ] Balanced use of rhetorical appeals (ethos, pathos, logos)
- [ ] Proactive objection handling (at least 2 potential concerns addressed)
- [ ] Clear call to action with specific next steps

**Structure Requirements:**

- [ ] Executive summary (150-200 words)
- [ ] Problem analysis section
- [ ] Proposed solution section
- [ ] Benefits and ROI section
- [ ] Implementation section
- [ ] Conclusion with call to action

**Format Requirements:**

- [ ] 800-1100 words (proposal body, excluding executive summary)
- [ ] 300-400 words (framework reflection, separate section after proposal)
- [ ] Professional proposal formatting
- [ ] Clear section headings and subheadings
- [ ] At least 3 credible sources cited

**Reflection Requirements:**

- [ ] Identify which Cialdini principles you applied and WHERE in your proposal
- [ ] Explain your rhetorical appeals strategy (balance of ethos, pathos, logos)
- [ ] Discuss how you applied Problem-Solution-Benefit structure
- [ ] Reflect on how Munter Framework and Stakeholder Mapping informed your audience adaptation
- [ ] Analyze what you learned about persuasive communication through framework application
