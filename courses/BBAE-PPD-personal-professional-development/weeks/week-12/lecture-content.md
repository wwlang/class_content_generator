**SLIDE 6: Personal Mastery - Senge's Framework**

CONTENT:

**Personal mastery** is the discipline of continually clarifying and deepening our personal vision, of focusing our energies, of developing patience, and of seeing reality objectively (Senge, 2006).

**Two key components:**

**1. Personal Vision** ‚Üí Who you want to become, what you want to create
- Not just career goals (external achievements)
- But your values, the person you aspire to be (internal development)

**2. Creative Tension** ‚Üí The gap between current reality and your vision
- This gap is GOOD - it creates energy for growth
- People with personal mastery use this tension productively
- They don't feel frustrated by the gap; they're energized by it

**Senge's insight:** Personal mastery is not something you "achieve" and finish. It's a lifelong discipline - a way of being, not a destination.

**Vietnamese context:** The Vietnamese proverb "H·ªçc, h·ªçc n·ªØa, h·ªçc m√£i" (Study, study more, study forever) - attributed to President Ho Chi Minh - reflects this same philosophy of lifelong learning and continuous self-improvement.

**SLIDE 7: Personal Vision vs. Goals - Understanding the Difference**

CONTENT:

**Many students confuse vision with goals. They're related but different:**

| Personal Vision | Goals |
|-----------------|-------|
| **WHO you want to become** | **WHAT you want to achieve** |
| Values-driven | Results-driven |
| Internal transformation | External accomplishment |
| Guides all decisions | Specific milestones |
| Relatively stable over time | Change as you progress |
| Example: "I want to be someone who creates positive impact through ethical business leadership" | Example: "Get MBA from top-ranked school by 2028" |

**The power of starting with vision:**

When your goals flow FROM your vision, they have deeper meaning. You're more resilient when facing obstacles because you're working toward who you want to BE, not just what you want to HAVE.

**Exercise (think quietly for 1 minute):**

**Integration point:** Look back at your VIA Character Strengths results from Week 5. Your top strengths are often clues to your authentic personal vision - the person you're naturally designed to become.

**Row 1: WHO vs WHAT**

**Row 2: Values-driven vs Results-driven**
"Vision is driven by your core values (justice, creativity, service, excellence)
Goals are driven by desired results (promotion, salary, title)
Neither is 'better' - but vision SHOULD drive goal selection."

**Row 3: Internal vs External**
"Internal transformation = changes in YOU (become more confident, develop patience, deepen expertise)
External accomplishment = changes in your CIRCUMSTANCES (job title, salary, office size)
You control internal. You only INFLUENCE external."

**Row 4: Guides vs Milestones**
"Vision guides EVERY decision: 'Should I take this job offer?' Check: Does it align with my vision of who I want to become?
Goals are specific milestones you can check off: 'Complete certification - DONE.' But then what? You need vision to set next goal."

**Row 5: Stable vs Changing**
"Vision tends to be stable across decades. My vision at 25: 'Help people learn and grow.' My vision at 50: same, but deeper.
Goals change constantly: 'Finish degree' ‚Üí 'Get first job' ‚Üí 'Earn promotion' ‚Üí 'Start business.' Goals SHOULD change as you grow."

**Example comparison:**
"Bad approach: 'My goal is to work at Google because it's prestigious and pays well.' (Pure goal, no vision)

**SLIDE 8: Connecting Values to Career Goals - The Alignment Framework**

CONTENT:

**The alignment framework** helps you check if your career goals truly match your values:

**Step 1: Identify Core Values**
- From Week 5 self-assessments: What matters most to you?
- VIA top strengths, MBTI preferences, mentor conversations
- Examples: Creativity, helping others, autonomy, security, achievement, innovation

**Step 2: Examine Career Goals**
- List your 1-year, 3-year, 5-year goals from Development Plan
- For EACH goal ask: "Why does this matter to me?"

**Step 3: Check Alignment**
- Do your goals express your values?
- Or are they based on others' expectations? (family pressure, social status, etc.)

**Step 4: Adjust for Authenticity**
- Redesign goals that don't align
- Add "how" strategies that honor your values
