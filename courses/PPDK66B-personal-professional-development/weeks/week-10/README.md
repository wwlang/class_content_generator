**Course:** Personal & Professional Development (PPDK66B)

**Type:** Experiential Learning Week

## Week Overview

Week 10 is the second experiential learning component of the course (following the MISA field trip in Week 6). Students will attend a guest speaker session with **Ms. Ha Linh** on "Career Journey & Lessons in the Digital Era," focusing on entrepreneurial mindset and career adaptability in Vietnam's evolving economy.

This session is a **mandatory component** of the Individual Assignment (Reflective Essay - 10%) and provides critical insights for the Personal Development Plan (40%).

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this week, students will be able to:

1. **Understand** the entrepreneurial mindset and how it applies to career success beyond starting a business
2. **Identify** key traits of entrepreneurial thinkers: innovation, calculated risk-taking, resilience, creative problem-solving, opportunity recognition, proactive initiative
3. **Apply** active listening and professional engagement skills during the guest speaker session
4. **Connect** insights from Ms. Ha Linh's career journey to personal career planning and development goals
5. **Reflect** on how entrepreneurial principles inform their Personal Development Plan
6. **Synthesize** observations from both MISA field trip (Week 6) and guest speaker to prepare for reflective essay
7. **Build** professional networking skills through Q&A participation and follow-up

## Common Questions

**Q: What if I miss the guest speaker session?**
A: Attendance is mandatory. This experience cannot be made up and is required for the Individual Assignment (Reflective Essay - 10%). If you have a legitimate emergency, contact your tutor immediately to explore limited alternatives.

**Q: How formal should my questions be?**
A: Professional but authentic. Avoid questions easily Googled or overly personal. Focus on actionable career insights. See preparation guide for question framework.

**Q: Do I have to ask a question publicly?**
A: While strongly encouraged, you're not penalized for not asking. However, professional engagement is valuable practice, and your prepared questions show thoughtful preparation.

**Q: How is this different from the MISA field trip?**
A: MISA showed workplace reality and organizational culture. Ms. Ha Linh shares personal career narrative and lessons learned. Together, they provide complementary perspectives on career development.

**Q: How long should the reflection template take?**
A: Budget 45-60 minutes for thoughtful, detailed completion. Quality reflection takes time and is essential for your essay and PDP.

**Q: Can I use AI to help with the reflection template?**
A: The reflection must be your authentic observations and insights. AI cannot capture YOUR specific experience, reactions, and learning. This is personal reflection, not generic content.

**Q: Should I connect with Ms. Ha Linh on LinkedIn?**
A: If you received her contact information and had meaningful interaction, a professional LinkedIn connection request is appropriate. Use the template provided in the reflection guide.
