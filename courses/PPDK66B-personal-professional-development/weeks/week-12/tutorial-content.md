## Quick Review

**Key concepts from today's lecture:**

- **Senge's Personal Mastery:** Creative tension between current reality and personal vision drives growth; avoid structural conflict (limiting beliefs); integrate reason with intuition; apply systems thinking
- **Self-Determination Theory (SDT):** Six types of motivation (amotivation ‚Üí intrinsic); three psychological needs (autonomy, competence, relatedness); identified regulation predicts performance better than pure intrinsic motivation
- **McKinsey's Seven Elements:** (1) Focus on growth, (2) Become serial master, (3) Stretch, (4) Build personal brand, (5) Own your development, (6) Do what you love (ikigai), (7) Stay vital
- **Course Synthesis:** 12-week journey from context ‚Üí learning tools ‚Üí self-knowledge ‚Üí experiences ‚Üí planning ‚Üí integration
- **Lifelong Commitment:** Annual reviews, quarterly check-ins, weekly reflection, continuous learning habits

- Clear articulation of self-discovery journey with specific assessment insights
- Integration of minimum 3 course concepts/frameworks across presentation
- SMART goals connected to values, strengths, and career vision
- Evidence of mentor influence throughout PDP

## Activity 3: Q&A Preparation & Practice (20 minutes)

**Assessment Connection:** The 5-minute Q&A is where examiners assess depth of understanding. Poor Q&A responses can significantly lower your grade even if presentation is strong.

**The Task:**

**Part A (10 min):** In pairs, practice answering examiner-style questions using the Question Bank below. Partner asks questions, you respond, partner provides feedback.

**Part B (10 min):** Identify your 3 most vulnerable areas (questions you struggle to answer). Develop strategies to strengthen responses.

**Deliverable:**

List of 3 vulnerable areas with prepared talking points.

**Success Criteria:**
- You practiced answering at least 6 different questions
- You can elaborate beyond surface-level responses
- You've identified potential weak spots and rehearsed answers

---

### Examiner Question Bank

**Common questions asked in Final Oral Exam:**

#### Self-Awareness & Assessment Questions:

1. "Your MBTI is [type] and Holland Code is [code]. How do these align or contradict? How did you reconcile differences?"

2. "You mentioned [VIA strength]. Can you give a specific example from this semester when you demonstrated this strength?"

3. "Looking at your assessment results, what surprised you most? What confirmed what you already knew?"

4. "How has your self-understanding changed from Week 1 to now?"

#### Goals & Planning Questions:

5. "Why did you choose [specific goal]? What if circumstances change and this goal becomes impossible?"

6. "Your 5-year goal is ambitious. What's your backup plan if you don't achieve it?"

7. "How do your 1-year goals build toward your 3-year goals? Show me the connection."

8. "You have goals in [area A] and [area B]. How will you balance competing priorities?"

9. "What will you do when you encounter [obstacle mentioned in PDP, e.g., limited financial resources, family expectations, job market challenges]?"

#### Course Integration Questions:

10. "How did the field trip to MISA influence your career thinking?"

11. "You mentioned growth mindset from Week 9. Give me an example of when you applied growth mindset during this course."

12. "How has your mentor's advice shaped your plan? Give me a specific example."

13. "We studied reflective practice in Week 3. How will you use Gibbs or Kolb's cycle in your career?"

14. "Connect your plan to the Future of Work concepts from Week 1-2. How are you preparing for the AI era?"

#### Commitment & Sustainability Questions:

15. "How will you prevent burnout pursuing these ambitious goals?" (Tests Element 7: Stay Vital)

16. "A year from now, how will you know if you're on track?"

17. "What will you do after this course to continue developing? Be specific about systems and habits."

18. "You mentioned [value, e.g., work-life balance]. How do your goals reflect this value?"

19. "What's the first action you'll take this week to move toward your goals?"

20. "In 5 years, if we meet again and you've achieved everything, what will you be most proud of?"

## Tips for Exam Success

### Content Tips:

1. **Tell YOUR story** ‚Äî This is YOUR development plan, make it personal and authentic
2. **Be specific** ‚Äî "My VIA top strength is Curiosity" > vague "I'm a learner"
3. **Connect everything** ‚Äî Show how assessments ‚Üí goals ‚Üí vision ‚Üí course learning all fit together
4. **Don't just list** ‚Äî Explain WHY it matters, not just WHAT you discovered
5. **Show growth** ‚Äî "I used to think X, but now I understand Y because of Z"

### Delivery Tips:

1. **Minimal text on slides** ‚Äî Visuals, keywords only; YOU provide the narrative
2. **Eye contact** ‚Äî Look at examiners, not screen/notes (know content so well you don't need to read)
3. **Vocal variety** ‚Äî Emphasize key points, pause for impact, vary pace
4. **Enthusiasm** ‚Äî This is YOUR future; show excitement and commitment
5. **Timing** ‚Äî Practice until you consistently hit 9:45-10:00; going over/under hurts score

### Q&A Tips:

1. **Pause before answering** ‚Äî 2-3 seconds to think shows thoughtfulness
2. **Elaborate, don't just answer yes/no** ‚Äî "Yes, and here's an example..."
3. **Connect to PDP** ‚Äî Reference specific sections of your plan
4. **Admit uncertainty gracefully** ‚Äî "I haven't fully worked that out yet, but my current thinking is..."
5. **Show you OWN the plan** ‚Äî Not memorized, genuinely YOURS

### Mindset Tips:

1. **Reframe nerves as excitement** ‚Äî Same physiological state, different interpretation (Week 9 growth mindset)
2. **Remember: Examiners want you to succeed** ‚Äî They're assessing, not attacking
3. **You're the expert on YOUR plan** ‚Äî No one knows it better than you
4. **Authenticity > perfection** ‚Äî A genuine, slightly imperfect presentation beats a polished but robotic one
5. **This is a celebration** ‚Äî You're showcasing 12 weeks of growth; be proud

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Content Mistakes:

- ‚ùå Reading assessment results without interpretation ("My MBTI is INTJ" with no insight)
- ‚ùå Listing goals without connection to values/strengths
- ‚ùå Vague statements ("I want to be successful" ‚Äî what does that mean?)
- ‚ùå No evidence of mentorship influence
- ‚ùå Generic presentation that could be anyone's plan

### Delivery Mistakes:

- ‚ùå Reading word-for-word from slides
- ‚ùå Turning back to screen instead of facing examiners
- ‚ùå Speaking too fast (nervous rushing)
- ‚ùå Going significantly over/under 10 minutes
- ‚ùå Monotone delivery (sounds bored with own plan)

### Q&A Mistakes:

- ‚ùå "I don't know" with no elaboration
- ‚ùå Defensive responses to probing questions
- ‚ùå Contradicting what you said in presentation
- ‚ùå Long, rambling answers (be concise but thorough)
- ‚ùå Making up answers instead of admitting you haven't considered that angle
